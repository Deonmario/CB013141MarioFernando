Horton Plains National Park

Nestled in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, Horton Plains National Park is characterized by its picturesque landscapes and unique biodiversity. The park is home to diverse ecosystems, including montane grasslands and cloud forests. Visitors can witness the iconic World's End, a sheer cliff with breathtaking views, and Baker's Falls, a mesmerizing waterfall.

Udawalawe National Park

Udawalawe National Park, situated in the southern part of Sri Lanka, is renowned for its large population of Asian elephants. The park's diverse habitats, including grasslands and water reservoirs, provide a haven for various wildlife species. A safari in Udawalawe offers opportunities to observe not only elephants but also a variety of birds, reptiles, and mammals.

Wildlife of Asia
Animal Name About Image
Sambur Deer Ambur deer are the largest deer species in Sri Lanka. They are known for their distinctive appearance with large, spiral-shaped antlers and a reddish-brown coat.
Purple-Faced Langur The purple-faced langur is an endemic primate species in Sri Lanka. It has a distinctively dark face with a purple hue and a long tail.
Sri Lankan Junglefowl The Sri Lankan Junglefowl is the national bird of Sri Lanka. Males have vibrant, multicolored plumage with striking red and blue markings.
